Quarterly Tax Strategy: A Calmer Approach

Zen Ledger Advisory June 9, 2026 5 min read

Annual tax planning, compressed into the weeks before a filing deadline, is structurally worse than the same work spread across four quarterly reviews. Decisions made under deadline pressure are rarely the same quality as decisions made with room to think.

A quarterly cadence allows for actions that simply are not available in December: quarterly estimated payment adjustments based on actual year-to-date performance, timing of major purchases or capital expenditures around known thresholds, and retirement contribution planning that benefits from being spread across the year rather than concentrated at the end.

It also surfaces problems early. An unexpected revenue swing identified in a Q2 review can be planned around calmly. The same swing, discovered for the first time while preparing a return in March, leaves far fewer options and far less time.

The quarterly review itself does not need to be lengthy — thirty focused minutes, four times a year, structured around the same handful of questions each time, produces meaningfully better outcomes than a single extended session under time pressure.
